# Hi there! I am Victor Santana.

I am a **Computer Engineering** student at **Brigham Young University - Idaho** with a **4.0 GPA**. Originally from the **Dominican Republic** and currently a **U.S. Lawful Permanent Resident**, my fascination with space, nuclear power, and embedded systems drives my goal to develop mission-critical automated systems. I am passionate about bridging the gap between high-level software architecture and reliable hardware integration.

## About Me

- **Current Focus:** I am dedicated to maximizing my final year as a student and engineering employee, ensuring I build a strong, practical foundation in hardware/software co-design before I graduate.
- **Goals:** As I prepare to graduate next year, my primary objective is to secure a second internship, transition into a full-time engineering job, or earn a position in a competitive Master's program.
- **IEEE Leadership:** Serving as the Vice Chair of the BYU-Idaho IEEE Student Branch, I bridge the gap between academia and industry by directing multi-disciplinary teams, organizing hands-on technical workshops, and executing professional networking events and industry site tours.
- **Hobbies:** I love working on older hardware and bringing it back to life. You can often find me refurbishing old tech to sell, tinkering with retro gaming consoles (GameCube, PS2, PS3), playing video games, camping, and spending quality time with my family and friends.
